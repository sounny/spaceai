AI for Earth Observation & Remote Sensing

Applications for Agricultural Development & Food Security

Eleanor Buchanan — International Space University · Content adapted from UN OOSA report

NASA
ISU

Abstract

Drawing on the UN Office for Outer Space Affairs' analysis of space technologies for agriculture, this poster summarizes how satellite data and AI together improve agricultural monitoring, strengthen early-warning systems for food security, and support evidence-based policy. The emphasis is on operational products, national capability building, and open-data strategies that enable timely, equitable access for decision-makers and communities.

Key Methods

  • Multisensor fusion (optical, SAR, thermal, microwave) to increase reliability and revisit
  • Time-series modelling for crop phenology, anomaly detection and yield estimation
  • Transfer learning and self-supervised methods to reduce labelled-data requirements
  • Uncertainty-aware AI and data assimilation for operational decision support

Applications in Agriculture

  • Crop-type mapping and acreage estimation for national statistics
  • Yield forecasting and seasonal outlooks to support food-security planning
  • Drought and flood early-warning using vegetation indices and soil-moisture products
  • Targeted advisory services and investment planning through high-resolution mapping

Policy & Capacity Highlights

  1. Promote open access to global EO datasets (e.g. Sentinel, Landsat) to reduce barriers
  2. Invest in national data infrastructure, processing pipelines and in-country training
  3. Co-design products with end-users and integrate in operational agricultural services
  4. Forge partnerships between space agencies, UN bodies, universities and local extension services
